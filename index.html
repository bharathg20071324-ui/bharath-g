1. Company Profile & Market Dominance

Life Insurance Corporation (LIC), established in 1956 by nationalizing 245 private entities, is India's largest insurer. With a mission to provide affordable insurance to the masses, it operates through a massive network of 13 lakh agents and 2,048 branch offices.

Post-IPO, the Government of India retains 96.5% ownership. LIC continues to dominate the sector despite private competition, holding a majority market share in New Business Premiums.

Equity Dominant Debt Free (Practical)

LIC maintains a unique structure with minimal debt. The Debt-Equity ratio remains extremely low (0.01 - 0.03), relying on internal accruals and policyholder funds.

5. Profitability Evolution

A massive transformation in margins. LIC shifted from low-margin/high-volume to high-margin products.
Key Insight: Net Profit Margin jumped from 0.72% (FY21) to 9.86% (FY25).

6. Peer Comparison (FY25)

Revenue Scale: LIC operates on a scale far beyond private peers. Its revenue of ₹4.88 Lakh Crore dwarfs SBI Life's ₹95k Crore.

Margin Leader: Surprisingly, LIC reported a higher Net Profit Margin (9.86%) in FY25 compared to private peers (~2.5%), largely due to accounting changes transferring surplus from policyholder funds.
